About Me

Researcher. Engineer. Problem Solver.

I am an M.Tech graduate in Artificial Intelligence and Data Science from the Indian Institute of Information Technology, Ranchi, with a CGPA of 8.85/10. My research sits at the intersection of Reinforcement Learning, Evolutionary Computation, and Quantum-inspired Optimization.

My primary focus has been the Dynamic Weapon–Target Assignment problem — a real-world NP-hard combinatorial challenge in autonomous defence, where I have developed novel ERL and Quantum-inspired PSO frameworks that outperform established baselines.

Prior to my Master's, I spent two years as a Project Engineer at Crio.Do building scalable EdTech infrastructure, and completed an internship at Infogain India developing enterprise Java backends.

I am actively seeking PhD opportunities in Artificial Intelligence, Optimization, and Autonomous Systems.

Research

What I Work On

RL · Evolutionary
Evolutionary RL for Dynamic Weapon–Target Assignment
  • Formulated DWTA as a sequential decision-making problem.
  • Proposed ERL framework integrating evolutionary search with RL.
  • Benchmarks generated via Taguchi orthogonal experimental design.
  • Compared against PPO, COERL, ERL, CCNCS and PDERL.
Quantum · UAV
Quantum-inspired PSO for Dynamic WTA
  • UAV-assisted Quantum-inspired PSO framework for DWTA.
  • Incorporated UAV mobility, weapon heterogeneity & probabilistic damage.
  • Superior convergence over GA, PSO, IPSO, IGA and QGA.
  • Large-scale combat simulation environments designed for evaluation.
Genetic · Metaheuristic
Modified Genetic Algorithm for Weapon–Target Allocation
  • Three-dimensional chromosome representation designed from scratch.
  • Customized crossover and mutation operators developed.
  • Improved convergence speed and solution quality over baselines.
Master's Thesis
Quantum-inspired Particle Swarm Optimization-based
Dynamic Weapon–Target Assignment
Developed a UAV-assisted Quantum-inspired Particle Swarm Optimization framework for solving Dynamic Weapon–Target Assignment under uncertainty by considering weapon heterogeneity, UAV mobility, probabilistic damage and mission constraints. The framework achieves superior convergence and damage efficiency compared to classical metaheuristic methods.
